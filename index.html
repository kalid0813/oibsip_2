Tribute to Mahatma Gandhi

1869-1948

Biograph

Mahatma Gandhi was the preeminent leader of India's independence movement against British colonial rule. Born in Porbandar, India, he studied law in London and practiced in South Africa, where he developed his philosophy of nonviolent resistance. Returning to India in 1915, he led peaceful protests and civil disobedience campaigns, including the famous Salt March. His principles of Ahimsa (nonviolence) and Satyagraha (truth force) inspired movements for civil rights and freedom worldwide. Gandhi's leadership led to India's independence in 1947. Tragically, he was assassinated in 1948, but his legacy endures as a symbol of peace, justice, and the power of nonviolent activism.
